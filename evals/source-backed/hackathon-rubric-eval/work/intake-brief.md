# Intake Brief

## Deck Goal

Help hackathon finalists build a short demo deck that judges can score quickly and fairly.

## Audience

Builders preparing for a judging session. They know their project, but they may not know how judges convert a short demo into a score.

## Format

- Live presentation.
- Three-minute target talk.
- Six-slide main deck.
- Q&A expected after the demo.
- HTML review deck with speaker notes.

## Benefit Statement

The audience should leave with a practical structure: rubric first, one clear demo path, proof of the hard part, and a fallback if the live demo fails.

## Proof Bar

External guidance from Devpost, MLH, and Y Combinator is enough for this eval deck because it teaches a presentation workflow rather than claims about a specific product.

## Source Mode

Allowed sources are the URLs and notes in `input/sources/hackathon-pitch-research.md`.

## Assumptions

- The hackathon has judging criteria or a judging process.
- The project team can provide at least one working demo or proof artifact.
- Any project-specific benchmark, user result, or model evaluation must be added later as a new claim.

## Stop Condition

Ready for human review after the deck renders, browser QA passes, and unresolved gaps are recorded in the review log.
