# Hackathon Pitch Research Notes

These notes are a compact source bundle for the source-backed eval deck. They are not a complete guide to every hackathon format.

## Sources

- Devpost Help: Judging and public voting
  - URL: https://help.devpost.com/hc/en-us/articles/360010137511-Judging-and-public-voting
  - Useful point: hackathon organizers define judging criteria; examples include quality of idea, implementation, and potential impact.

- MLH Organizer Guide: Judging Plan
  - URL: https://guide.mlh.io/general-information/judging-and-submissions/judging-plan
  - Useful point: judging should be planned as an explicit event process, including submissions, judging flow, and judge communication.

- Y Combinator: Demo Day Pitch Guide
  - URL: https://www.ycombinator.com/library/2u-demo-day-pitch-guide
  - Useful point: a short pitch should create enough interest for the next conversation rather than trying to prove everything.

- Y Combinator: How to Pitch Your Company
  - URL: https://www.ycombinator.com/library/4T-how-to-pitch-your-company
  - Useful point: the pitch should quickly make the audience understand what the product does, who it is for, and why it matters.

## Synthesis For This Eval

The deck should recommend a rubric-first approach, not because every hackathon has the same criteria, but because hackathon judging is usually criteria-driven and time-constrained. The deck should advise teams to select proof that helps judges score the project: a crisp problem, a working demo path, technical evidence for the hard part, and a fallback asset if the live demo fails.
