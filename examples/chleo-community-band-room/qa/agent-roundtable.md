# Agent Roundtable

## Roles Used

- Founder lens: what Chleo would worry about before signing a lease.
- Real-estate lens: how to scout, negotiate and avoid unusable premises.
- Music-operator lens: what musicians, teachers and studio users will challenge.
- Red-team lens: accuracy, legality, cost and overclaiming risks.

## Consensus

- The proposal should help Chleo start a studio step by step, rather than describe an attractive room.
- The first version should be private rehearsal, lessons and closed workshops. Public showcases are a later phase after approvals.
- The first major decision is not decor or branding. It is whether demand, site approval, noise survivability and lease terms clear before fixed costs start.
- A partner pilot is the safest first move if Chleo does not yet have recurring users. An owned best-value room is the target after proof.
- Cheap rent can be misleading. Industrial space may be cheap, but it can fail the use-approval and customer-facing activity test.
- Musicians will care more about maintained mid-tier gear, a tuned kit, good vocal sound, clean cables and predictable booking than boutique gear.
- Drums are the risk center. Treatment helps the room sound better; isolation controls leakage and usually means construction.

## Disagreements Resolved

- The founder lens wanted the deck to feel possible. The red-team lens wanted stronger caveats. Resolution: make risks practical gates, not fear language.
- The real-estate lens warned against industrial as the default. Resolution: show industrial as a fallback only after written approval and 60:40 checks.
- The operator lens wanted more room-experience detail. Resolution: add teacher mode, band mode, maintained gear, setup rules and drum/noise discipline to the main deck.

## Copy Rules

- No corny slogans or motivational filler.
- No blanket "legal" claims without a real address and approval path.
- No "soundproofing" unless the slide means isolation construction.
- No claim that Singapore lacks affordable jamming rooms.
- Keep Chleo's action path clear: prove demand, clear the site, build only what can be sustained.
