# Agent Critiques

## Music-Studio Operator And Musician Lens

### What Feels Convincing

- The proposal is strongest when it sells reliability: fast plug-in rehearsal, a tuned drum kit, dependable vocal monitoring, clean handover, and no mystery cables.
- Teachers will believe the concept if daytime use is explicit: lesson blocks, teacher residencies, storage, parent-safe access, and cancellation rules.
- The community angle works when it is translated into operating behavior: beginner-friendly sessions, hosted jams, low-pressure showcases after licensing checks, and clear norms.

### What Feels Naive Or Missing

- Do not imply foam panels solve leakage. Treatment improves room sound; isolation is construction.
- Casual jamming alone is too weak as a business engine for a one-room studio in Singapore.
- Under-buying small items will hurt the experience: mic stands, DI boxes, spare cables, drum stool, music stands, tuner, spare sticks, patch leads, tools, and repair reserve.

### Corrections To Slides

- Add a room-standard block: tuned kit, two guitar amps, bass amp or DI, three to four vocal mics, digital mixer or simple analog mixer, PA, monitoring, music stands, access policy, and cleaning checklist.
- Use "welcoming but run with rules" as the positioning line instead of the broader word "community".
- Keep showcases out of the launch promise until site use, SCDF, SPF, IMDA/NAC, COMPASS and MRSS checks are confirmed.

## Aspiring Founder Lens

### What Feels Convincing

- The staged launch path makes the plan less risky: prove demand, partner first if needed, then commit to one owned best-value room.
- The proposal should name the emotional reason: Chleo wants a room where young musicians feel invited instead of judged.
- The 90-day path should feel like a founder checklist, not a contractor checklist.

### What Feels Naive Or Missing

- A founder will underestimate working capital, repairs, deposits, bad bookings, and slow weekdays.
- "Not too expensive" must be protected by pre-sold recurring hours. Cheap hourly pricing without recurring utilization will create stress.
- Community can become messy if boundaries are unclear.

### Corrections To Slides

- Add a pre-sell target before lease commitment: teacher partners, member passes, and recurring band blocks.
- Add a "first 10 customers" section in the 90-day path.
- Make the final decision ask specific: choose pilot, own-room, or showcase-ready path and approve the first validation budget.

## Real-Estate Scout Lens

### What Feels Convincing

- The deck is right to treat the premises as the make-or-break decision.
- A good search brief is: approved or approvable music school/rehearsal use, landlord written consent, late access, air-con capacity, low neighbour sensitivity, practical loading, and permission for acoustic doors/treatment.

### What Feels Naive Or Missing

- Cheap industrial rent is not automatically suitable. JTC/URA 60:40, surrounding use compatibility, landlord consent, SCDF, NEA and MCST/building rules can block the plan.
- Lease cash includes more than monthly rent. Plan for deposit, first month, stamp duty, legal/admin costs, renovation deposit, utilities deposit, insurance, reinstatement, and rent-free fitout negotiation.
- Do not sign based on agent verbal assurance. Ask for approved use, landlord consent, and authority pathway in writing.

### Corrections To Slides

- Add a five-gate site checklist before cost slides.
- Add negotiation levers: rent-free fitout period, capped reinstatement, right to install acoustic works, after-hours access, early termination if approvals fail, deposit level, and approval contingency.
- Separate "commercial music-school fit" from "industrial low-rent trap" in the options table.
