# Visual Aid Catalog

Choose visual aids based on the concept, not decoration.

## Comparison

- `was_is`: old workflow vs current/new workflow.
- `before_after`: same scenario before and after intervention.
- `old_world_new_world`: strategic contrast.
- `manual_assisted_autonomous`: AI progression.
- `side_by_side_methods`: compare approaches such as RLHF vs DPO.
- `complexity_comparison`: show fewer steps, fewer models, lower cost, or fewer failure points.

## Mechanism

- `pipeline`: stages with inputs and outputs.
- `runtime_flow`: execution path through services or agents.
- `loop_map`: retry, repair, reflection, or feedback loop.
- `state_machine`: states and transitions.
- `probability_shift`: before/after distributions.
- `math_stepper`: formula revealed in steps.
- `decision_tree`: choose a tool or path by constraints.

## Teaching

- `analogy_bridge`: everyday analogy mapped to technical concept.
- `misconception_dialogue`: short dialogue to correct a likely misunderstanding.
- `concrete_example`: one specific prompt, user, transaction, or document.
- `worked_example`: numbers change step by step.

## Evidence

- `benchmark_matrix`: tool or method comparison with sourced metrics.
- `rubric_scorecard`: weighted criteria and scores.
- `measured_vs_published`: separate local measurements from external benchmarks.
- `source_trace`: show where a claim came from.

## Product Demo

- `phone_mockup`: mobile flow, especially for user-facing risk or decision moments.
- `browser_scene`: product UI in realistic context.
- `before_after_user_journey`: user path without and with the product.
- `code_snippet`: short proof of mechanism from actual code.

## Selection Rule

If the audience might ask "how?", use a mechanism visual. If they might ask "why better?", use a comparison. If they might ask "can I trust it?", use evidence or source trace. If they might ask "what happens to me?", use a scenario or product demo.

## Removal Rule

A slide may have no major visual aid. Remove a visual aid when it does not change what the audience understands, when it repeats the title, or when it makes the slide harder to present. Use whitespace, a strong title, or one precise example instead.
